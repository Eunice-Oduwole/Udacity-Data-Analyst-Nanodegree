Effect of Bike Hiring Characteristics on Trip Duration¶

by Eunice Oduwole¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I wanted to look at the characteristics of bike hiring that could be used to predict trip duration. The main focus was on the day of the week, user type (subscriber or customer i.e casual user) and hours of the day.

Dataset Overview¶

The consist of trip duration and attributes of about 192082 entries. The attribute included four feature, with other attribute such as bike_share_for_all_trip, start_station_name, start station id. Two more features were added such that we have entries for time in term of day and time (extracted from the start time column).

Distribution of trip durations¶

The trip duration had a large range of values, from about 0 at the lowest to about 7800 at the highest. Thus, a plot was made use a logarithmic scale and the distribution appear to be skewed to the right.

Distribution of hours of the day¶

The distribution of the hour of day is roughly bimodal, with the first peak roughly between 8:00 and 10:00. There is a sharp drop in the trip frequency after, this could probably be because people are now settled for the day's activities such as office work, school etc. Again the trip start increase at about 14:00 and reaches it's second peak about 16:00 and gradually decreases. Again, this could be attributed to fact that people are closed for the day and take this bike trip to there various destinations.
